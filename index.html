    Authentication · Operational Efficiency

    Branch Passcode Authentication

    📍 Situation

    In-branch customer service representatives were spending disproportionate time on identity verification procedures—manual checks that felt like security theater to both reps and customers. This created wait times, customer frustration, and prevented reps from focusing on solving actual customer problems.

    🎯 Task

    Design a lightweight authentication feature for in-person branches that simplifies identity verification while maintaining security. Ship within a single PI cycle (4–5 sprints). Success = measurable time savings for customer service reps and positive customer feedback.

    ⚡ Action

    I championed simplicity over complexity. We built a minimalist solution: Angular UI for customers to generate time-limited passcodes, verified via Transmit Security backend, stored securely in our identity system. The whole feature including release planning and client communication took 5 sprints to ship. I partnered closely with branch operations teams to understand their workflows and ensure the feature fit seamlessly into their processes.

    ✅ Result

    Customer service representatives saved 283.5 hours annually (equivalent to ~$40K in labor costs). Customers reported measurably higher satisfaction with in-branch experience. The feature became a template for other in-person authentication scenarios. Key lesson: highest-impact programs aren't always the most technically complex—they're the ones that solve real problems for real people.

    Regulatory Compliance · Security

    MFA Hardstop — NYDFS Compliance

    📍 Situation

    NYDFS (New York Department of Financial Services) issued a regulatory mandate: all financial institutions must deploy mandatory multi-factor authentication by a specific deadline, or face financial penalties and license suspension. Our organization was significantly behind on this initiative, with no clear path to compliance.

    🎯 Task

    Lead the MFA implementation program under a compressed timeline. Success = ship on the regulatory deadline without a single security incident or customer-facing disruption. Additional goal: use this as an opportunity to modernize authentication architecture, not just check a compliance box.

    ⚡ Action

    I took ownership and reframed the deadline as a strategic opportunity. I coordinated engineering, product, legal, compliance, and operations—teams that rarely worked together. Critically, I championed LaunchDarkly for feature flag management, enabling a phased rollout strategy: QA/staging validation → internal users → customer LOBs (wealth, banking, brokerage) → 100% production traffic. This approach reduced deployment risk dramatically. I championed security rigor while pushing for velocity and provided executive visibility through weekly status dashboards.

    ✅ Result

    Shipped on regulatory deadline with zero missed commitments. Zero security incidents or customer disruptions. Reduced deployment-related incidents by 40% through LaunchDarkly's phased rollout strategy. Beyond compliance, the program yielded strategic wins: became the foundation for enterprise-grade security features, strengthened regulator relationships, and proved cross-functional teams could deliver excellence under pressure.

    IAM · Cloud Migration

    Login Management Modernization

    📍 Situation

    A Fortune 500 financial services firm faced a critical bottleneck: their aging authentication infrastructure was becoming a liability. Legacy systems couldn't handle demand spikes during peak hours, were difficult to secure against evolving threats, and created friction for both web and mobile customers trying to access their accounts.

    🎯 Task

    Lead a full-scale modernization program to migrate to a cloud-native identity platform. The challenge: coordinate across security, compliance, product, and engineering teams while maintaining zero downtime for 50M+ active users—any authentication failure is a business-critical incident.

    ⚡ Action

    I architected the migration in phases, minimizing risk at each step. We selected Transmit Security for multi-journey authentication (web, mobile, branch) and deployed to Google Cloud for enterprise-grade reliability. I established weekly coordination meetings across all stakeholders, created clear decision rights, and maintained executive dashboards showing progress and risk. We built comprehensive monitoring and automated rollback capabilities. I championed testing rigor—every change was validated against the entire user journey before hitting production.

    ✅ Result

    100% uptime post-migration with 300K requests/minute sustained throughput. Enterprise-grade security posture with MFA, SAML, and OIDC support. This modernization became the foundation for all future identity-driven features and regulatory compliance initiatives. No customer-facing incidents. Timeline: on schedule.

    Digital Transformation · Cost Savings

    Digital Self-Serve Mechanism

    📍 Situation

    A critical business pain point: customer service teams were spending 30–40% of their time handling repetitive manual account management requests that could easily be automated. These bottlenecks created operational inefficiencies, longer wait times for customers, and frustration for both teams.

    🎯 Task

    Design and deliver a self-serve platform that eliminates manual intervention while improving customer satisfaction. Success metrics: reduce support volume by at least 50%, generate measurable cost savings, and achieve high customer adoption within one quarter.

    ⚡ Action

    I led a full-stack engineering effort: Angular frontend for intuitive UX, Spring Boot backend for reliability, MongoDB for flexible data modeling. We deployed on Google Cloud with careful phasing to ensure adoption and minimize risk. I partnered with customer service to design workflows that matched their mental models. We invested heavily in onboarding and support so teams felt confident promoting the new tool.

    ✅ Result

    $2M+ in annualized cost savings through reduced headcount demand. Zero new hires required despite 15% growth in user base—the platform absorbed all incremental volume. Support volume dropped 60%. Measurable improvements in customer satisfaction scores. This project became an internal case study for how modern engineering practices translate directly to business value.
